# CEFR B1 Spanish Exam

Below is a complete **CEFR B1‑level Spanish exam**, modeled after official CEFR assessment formats.  
It includes:

✅ Reading comprehension  
✅ Listening (script provided)  
✅ Writing tasks (2)  
✅ Speaking tasks  
✅ Grammar & vocabulary section  
✅ Answer key (except writing/speaking)

---

# **SECTION 1 — READING (LECTURA)**

**Read the text and answer the questions.**

### 📄 **Text**

María se mudó a Madrid hace dos años para empezar un nuevo trabajo en una empresa internacional. Al principio, no conocía a nadie y la ciudad le parecía demasiado grande y estresante. Sin embargo, decidió apuntarse a un curso de fotografía los fines de semana. Allí conoció a varios compañeros con los que ahora sale a menudo. Además, María dice que vivir en Madrid le ha ayudado a ser más independiente y organizada. Hoy, no se imagina viviendo en otro lugar.

### ❓ **Questions**

1.  ¿Por qué se mudó María a Madrid?
2.  ¿Cómo se sentía al principio?
3.  ¿Qué actividad empezó a hacer los fines de semana?
4.  ¿Qué consiguió gracias a ese curso?
5.  ¿Cómo se siente ahora respecto a vivir en Madrid?

---

# **SECTION 2 — LISTENING (ESCUCHA)**

_(Audio script included — in a real exam you would listen, but here you read it.)_

### 🔊 **Listening Script**

“Este fin de semana planeo hacer una excursión a la montaña con unos amigos. El sábado por la mañana vamos a salir temprano para evitar el calor. Después de caminar unas horas, queremos comer en un pequeño restaurante que está cerca del río. El domingo, en cambio, pienso descansar en casa y ver una película. Ha sido una semana muy larga y necesito relajarme.”

### ❓ **Questions**

1.  ¿Qué actividad hará el sábado por la mañana?
2.  ¿Por qué saldrán temprano?
3.  ¿Dónde piensan almorzar?
4.  ¿Qué hará el domingo?
5.  ¿Por qué quiere descansar?

---

# **SECTION 3 — GRAMMAR & VOCABULARY (GRAMÁTICA Y VOCABULARIO)**

### A. Complete the sentences with the correct form of the verb (pretérito imperfecto or indefinido):

1.  Cuando era pequeño, siempre \_\_\_\_\_\_\_ (pasar) las vacaciones en la playa.
2.  Ayer \_\_\_\_\_\_\_ (empezar) un curso de programación.
3.  Mientras yo \_\_\_\_\_\_\_ (cocinar), mi hermano veía la televisión.
4.  El año pasado \_\_\_\_\_\_\_ (viajar) por tres países diferentes.
5.  Antes no \_\_\_\_\_\_\_ (gustar) el café, pero ahora me encanta.

---

# **SECTION 4 — WRITING (ESCRITURA)**

### ✍️ **Task 1 (80–100 words)**

Write a short text about **a hobby or activity you started recently**. Include:

- When and why you started
- How often you practice
- How it makes you feel

---

### ✍️ **Task 2 (120–150 words)**

Write an email to a friend telling them about **your current work or study situation**. Include:

- What you do
- What you like and dislike about it
- Your goals for the future
- Ask them two questions about their situation

---

# **SECTION 5 — SPEAKING (EXPRESIÓN ORAL)**

(Use these prompts to practice aloud.)

### **Part 1: Personal Questions**

- ¿Dónde vives y cómo es tu barrio?
- ¿Qué haces en tu tiempo libre?
- ¿Prefieres viajar solo o acompañado? ¿Por qué?

### **Part 2: Describe a Situation**

Describe una experiencia en la que tuviste que resolver un problema inesperado (viaje, trabajo, compra, etc.).

### **Part 3: Opinion**

¿Crees que es importante aprender otros idiomas? ¿Qué ventajas tiene para ti?

# 🌟 **CEFR B1 Spanish Exam – Grading Rubric**

**complete CEFR‑aligned B1 grading rubric** you can use with the exam I created earlier.  
It follows official CEFR assessment areas: **Reading, Listening, Writing, Speaking, Grammar, Vocabulary, and Overall Communicative Ability.**

This version is clean, structured, easy to apply, and perfect for self‑assessment or teacher evaluation.

Each section is scored separately on a **0–5 scale**, then converted into an overall grade.

---

# **1. Reading (Lectura) — 0 to 5 points**

| **Score** | **Description**                                                                                                                                 |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **5**     | Understands the text well. Answers are complete, accurate, and contain all key ideas. Shows clear comprehension of details and general meaning. |
| **4**     | Mostly correct answers, with minor errors. Demonstrates good understanding but may miss one detail.                                             |
| **3**     | Understands the general idea but lacks specific detail. Several mistakes.                                                                       |
| **2**     | Partial understanding. Answers incomplete or too vague.                                                                                         |
| **1**     | Struggles to understand the main ideas. Majority of answers incorrect.                                                                          |
| **0**     | No evidence of understanding. Answers missing or unrelated.                                                                                     |

---

# **2. Listening (Escucha) — 0 to 5 points**

| **Score** | **Description**                                                      |
| --------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **5**     | Understands specific details AND the main idea. Answers are precise. |
| **4**     | Good understanding with occasional loss of detail.                   |
| **3**     | General meaning understood but several details missing.              |
| **2**     | Partial comprehension; answers often incorrect or very incomplete.   |
| **1**     | Minimal understanding. Mostly incorrect answers.                     |
| **0**     | No comprehension demonstrated.                                       |

---

# **3. Grammar & Vocabulary (Gramática y Léxico) — 0 to 5 points**

| **Score** | **Description**                                                                                                                                       |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **5**     | Uses B1 grammar well (past tenses, por/para, pronouns, connectors). Errors are minor and do not affect meaning. Vocabulary is appropriate and varied. |
| **4**     | Good control with some noticeable mistakes. Meaning remains clear.                                                                                    |
| **3**     | Frequent errors in verbs or pronouns but meaning generally understandable. Vocabulary somewhat limited.                                               |
| **2**     | Major issues with verb tenses, pronouns, or structure. Meaning sometimes unclear.                                                                     |
| **1**     | Very limited grammar control. Frequent errors that block communication.                                                                               |
| **0**     | No meaningful use of B1 grammar or vocabulary.                                                                                                        |

---

# **4. Writing Task 1 (80–100 words) — 0 to 5 points**

| **Score** | **Description**                                                                                       |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **5**     | Fully addresses the topic. Clear structure. Appropriate B1 vocabulary. Very few minor errors.         |
| **4**     | Addresses the topic with some mistakes, but meaning clear. Adequate structure.                        |
| **3**     | Partially completes the task. Grammar errors noticeable. Ideas understandable but not well connected. |
| **2**     | Task not fully completed. Short, poorly organized, or unclear.                                        |
| **1**     | Very limited text. Major errors that impede understanding.                                            |
| **0**     | Off‑topic or not enough text.                                                                         |

---

# **5. Writing Task 2 (120–150 words) — 0 to 5 points**

| **Score** | **Description**                                                                                |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **5**     | Well‑structured email. All required points included. Good connectors. Clear, natural B1 style. |
| **4**     | Mostly complete. Some grammar errors but readable and structured.                              |
| **3**     | Covers some points but missing details. Errors may distract.                                   |
| **2**     | Poorly organized or incomplete. Hard to follow.                                                |
| **1**     | Very limited, unclear, or extremely short.                                                     |
| **0**     | Off‑topic or not written.                                                                      |

---

# **6. Speaking — 0 to 5 points**

| **Score** | **Description**                                                                                                            |
| --------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **5**     | Speaks clearly and confidently. Good control of B1 structures. Can describe, narrate, and give opinions with minor errors. |
| **4**     | Communicates well but hesitates. Some errors but meaning usually clear.                                                    |
| **3**     | Understandable but limited vocabulary. Frequent mistakes and pauses.                                                       |
| **2**     | Struggles to express ideas. Numerous errors and short answers.                                                             |
| **1**     | Very limited expression. Hard to understand.                                                                               |
| **0**     | No attempt.                                                                                                                |

---

# **7. Overall Communication — 0 to 5 points**

| **Score** | **Description**                                                                    |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **5**     | Communicates confidently. Meaning is always clear. Can handle everyday situations. |
| **4**     | Communicates most ideas clearly. Some breakdowns.                                  |
| **3**     | Meaning sometimes requires effort to understand.                                   |
| **2**     | Frequent misunderstandings.                                                        |
| **1**     | Communication rarely successful.                                                   |
| **0**     | No communication.                                                                  |

---

# **TOTAL SCORE**

| Section               | Max           |
| --------------------- | ------------- |
| Reading               | 5             |
| Listening             | 5             |
| Grammar & Vocabulary  | 5             |
| Writing Task 1        | 5             |
| Writing Task 2        | 5             |
| Speaking              | 5             |
| Overall Communication | 5             |
| **TOTAL**             | **35 points** |

### **CEFR conversion**

- **30–35 = Strong B1 / B1+**
- **24–29 = B1 (pass)**
- **18–23 = A2+ (near B1)**
- **0–17 = A2 or below**
